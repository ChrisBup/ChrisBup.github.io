Bullet Points Tour

-1661 - Boswijck - 1 of 6 townships -would become the brewing capital -1664 - Dutch are expelled -1770s - New York Town and Philadelphia are colonial brewing centers 1776 first Germans in Brooklyn; foreshadowing. -1783 - The British are expelled -cider and sherry, they also drank beer; -safer than water; consumed by all ages, all times of day -George Washington brewed porter as did the original hipster, Thomas Jefferson; claimed to not use a recipe -"The Theory and Practice of Brewing" by Michael Combrune

-1822 - William Johnson; first in Brooklyn, on Front Street -1840 - John Wagner from Bavaria brought lager beer yeast to Philadelphia -2 years later, Schaefer would open, introducing one of the first lagers to New York City -1848 - failed revolution, Industrial Revolution -1855 - Brooklyn consolidates

-1858 - The Ridgewood Reservoir opens in Highland Park and feeds fresh water into Bushwick; for beer and life -big reason why this area was where people settled -up to this point, they got locals would get their water from farther out on Long Island; ponds, streams, eastern Kings, southern Queens -supply quickly depleted, wells turned salty and the water table dropped significantly, farmers threatened -Croton Aqueduct across the river -water is an essential part of beer. 4–5 gallons water per gallon of beer -water can have a flavor composition all its own (from varying levels of minerals, pH, etc.) but it also supports yeast, which then eats sugar and turns it into alcohol. Yeast need manganese, zinc, copper and iron to survive and thrive—it ensures a healthful fermentation. These are all reasons why a town's water supply has long determined what the local beer tastes like. Terroir, the characteristic flavor that wine gets from the land the grapes were grown on, does not only apply to wine. -When processing and filtering water was difficult, people put breweries where there were great water supplies. So the fact that so many breweries sprung up in this area, tells you how successful that reservoir was.
